Ramanujan sums

Contents

  • Relation with sum of divisors

Let \(e(x) = e^{2 \pi i x}\).

We define the Ramunjan sum \(c_q(n)\) to be \[\begin{align} c_q(n) = \sum_{ \substack{ a \pmod{q} \\ (a,q) =1 } } e\left( \tfrac{a}{q}n \right). \end{align}\]

Relation with sum of divisors

Let \(\sigma(n)\) be the sum of divisors function. We have the relation \[\begin{align} \sigma_{k-1}(n) = n^{k-1} \zeta(k) \sum_{q=1}^{\infty} \frac{c_q(n)}{q^{k}}. \end{align}\]
