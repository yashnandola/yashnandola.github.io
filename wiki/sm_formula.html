Sherman-Morrison Formula

Contents

  • Example

This is the name of the following matrix identity.

For \(A \in GL_{n}(\mathbb{R})\) and \(u,v \in \mathbb{R}^{n}\) (written as column vectors), we have

\[\begin{align} \left(A + uv^\textsf{T}\right)^{-1} = A^{-1} - {A^{-1}uv^\textsf{T}A^{-1} \over 1 + v^\textsf{T}A^{-1}u}. \end{align}\]

For example, when \(A = I\), the identity matrix, we have

\[\begin{align} \left(I + uv^\textsf{T}\right)^{-1} = I - \frac{uv^\textsf{T}}{1 + v^\textsf{T}u}. \end{align}\]

The determinant is the denomintor of the second term, in both the formulas above.

Example

In many cases, we have to compute inverse of a matrix

\[\begin{align} A= \begin{bmatrix} 1+a & 1 & 1 &1 & \ldots &1\\ 1 & 1+a & 1 & 1 & \ldots &1 \\ 1 & 1 & 1+a & 1 & \ldots &1 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \\ 1 & 1 & 1& 1 & & 1+a \end{bmatrix} = a I + vv^{T}, \end{align}\] where \(v\) is the column vectors with all \(1\)s.

According to the above formula, the inverse of this is \[\begin{align} A^{-1} = \tfrac{1}{a}I - \frac{\tfrac{1}{a^2}v v^{T}}{(1+\tfrac{1}{a}v^{T}v)} = \frac{(a+n)I - vv^{T}}{a(a+n)}. \end{align}\]
