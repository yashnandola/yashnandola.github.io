A division algebra over a field \(k\) is a \(k\)-algebra such that it satisfies all the axioms of a field except commutativity.

For a finite dimensional \(\mathbb{Q}\)-division algebra \(D\), \(D\otimes_{\mathbb{Q}} \mathbb{R}\) is always a real semisimple algebra as given there. This is the subject of [1] and [2].

Siegel mean value theorem

See Siegel mean value theorem for a division algebra variant of the Siegel mean value theorem.

Gram-Schmidt algorithm

A type of Gram-Schmidt process can be done for the \(D_{\mathbb{R}}\).

Minkowski’s theorem

The Minkowski’s theorem on successive minima can be generalized to the setting of free \(D_\mathbb{R}\)-modules.
