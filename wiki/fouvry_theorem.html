Fouvry’s theorem

The following result is due to Fouvry, based on a work of Katz and Laumon. [1] We state the main theorem produced therein.

Let \(X\) be a integral subscheme of \(\mathbb{Z}^{d}\) with the property that any irreducible component of \(X(\mathbb{C})\) is of dimension \(\le k\) and such that no irreducible component of dimension \(k\) of \(X(\mathbb{C})\) is contained in a hyperplane of \(\mathbb{C}^{d}\).

Then, if we identify the points \(\mathbb{F}_{p}\) with the points \(\mathbb{Z}\cap [0,p-1]\), and consider \(X(\mathbb{F}_p) \subseteq \mathbb{F}_p^{d}\), then \[\begin{align} \# X(\mathbb{F}_p) \cap [0,x]^d &\sim \# X(\mathbb{F}_p) \left( \frac{x}{p}\right)^{d}, \end{align}\] provided that \(x \gg p^{\frac{1}{2(d-k+1)}} \log p\) as \(p \rightarrow \infty\).

References

1.
É. Fouvry, Consequences of a result of N. Katz and G. Laumon concerning trigonometric sums. Israel Journal of Mathematics, 120 (2000) 81–96.
