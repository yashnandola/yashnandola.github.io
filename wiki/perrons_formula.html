Perron’s Formula

Contents

  • References

Let \(x \in \mathbb{R}\). Then, we have that for \(c \in \mathbb{R}\) \[\begin{align} \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{c-i \infty }^{c + i \infty} e^{xs} \frac{ds}{s} = \begin{cases}1 & x > 0\\ \tfrac{1}{2} & x=0 \\ 0 & x < 0 \end{cases}. \end{align}\]

The implication of this lemma is the following theorem of Perron. This version is as stated in [1]. Proof can be found therein.

(Perron) Let \(f(s) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_{i} e^{-\lambda_i s}\) be a general Dirichlet series. This means that \(a_i,s \in \mathbb{C}\) and \(\lambda_i\) is a strictly increasing sequence of real numbers.

Let \(\sigma \in \mathbb{R}\) be such that the Dirichlet series of \(f(s)\) is convergent for \(\Re(s) > \sigma\). Let \(c \in \mathbb{R}\) be such that \(c > 0\) and \(c > \sigma\). Let \(w \in \mathbb{R}\) be such that \(\lambda_n < w < \lambda_{n+1}\). Then \[\begin{align} \frac{1}{2 \pi i} \int_{c - i \infty}^{c+i \infty} f(s)e^{ws} \frac{ds}{s} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_{i}. \end{align}\]
