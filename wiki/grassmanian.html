For a field \(K\), the Grassmannian variety \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Gr}}}(m,K^{n})\) is an algebraic variety which as a set is equal to the set of all \(m\)-dimensional \(K\)-subspaces of \(K^{n}\). This is variety is the subject of [1].

Schmidt’s theorem

Height’s on susbpaces

Let us recreate the height function on \(K\)-subspaces of \(K^{t}\) as given in [1].

Consider the standard Pl"uker embedding \[\begin{align} \iota: \mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Gr}}}(m,K^n) & \rightarrow \mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{P}}}\left({ \wedge}^{m} K^{n} \right)\\ \mathop{\mathrm{span}}_{K}(w_1,w_2,\dots,w_m) & \mapsto [ w_1 \wedge w_2 \wedge \dots \wedge w_m ] . \end{align}\] Here \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{P}}}({ \wedge}^{m}K^{n})=\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Gr}}}(1,{ \wedge}^{m}K^n)\) is the \(m\)th exterior product (over \(K\)) of the vector space \(K^{n}\) and \(w_1,\dots,w_m\) are some \(K\)-linearly independent vectors inside \(K^{n}\). A constructive way to see this map is that if \(S \in \mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Gr}}}(m,K^{n})\) is generated by \(w_1,\dots,w_m\) then \(\iota(S)\) is the one-dimensional subspace generated by the \(m \times m\) minors of the $n m $ matrix whose columns are \(w_1,\dots,w_m\).

Let \(\sigma_1,\sigma_2,\dots,\sigma_{[K:\mathbb{Q}]} : K \rightarrow \mathbb{C}\) be all the complex embeddings of \(K\) into \(\mathbb{C}\). We can apply them coordinate wise and lift them as \(\sigma_1, \dots , \sigma_{N} : K^{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{C}^{N}\) for any \(N \ge 1\). Now, for any projective space \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{P}}}(K^{N})\), we can define the \(l^2\)-height function as

\[\begin{align} H: \mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{P}}}(K^{N}) \rightarrow & \ \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}\\ % S \mapsto & \ H(S) \\ [x_1,\dots,x_N ] \mapsto & \frac{1}{\N( \langle x_1,x_2,\dots,x_N \rangle_{\mathcal{O}_K} ) } \prod_{i=1}^{[K:\mathbb{Q}]} \sqrt{\sum_{j=1}^{N} |\sigma_i(x_j)|^{2}}. \end{align}\] Here the denominator is the norm of the fractional ideal in \(\mathcal{O}_K\) generated by \(x_1,\dots,x_N \in K\). Observe that this is a well-defined function on \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{P}}}(K^{N})\)

Using any suitable enumeration of the size-\(m\) subsets of \(\{1,2,\dots,n\}\), we get an obvious identification \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{P}}}({ \wedge}^{m} K^{n} ) \leftrightarrow \mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{P}}}(K^{\binom{n}{m}})\). Using this, we can define the height of a subspace in \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Gr}}}(m,K^{n})\) to be \[\begin{align} H: \mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Gr}}}(m,K^{n}) \rightarrow & \ \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}\\ S \mapsto & \ H(\iota(S)) \\ \label{eq:defi_of_height} \end{align}\]

Here is Theorem 3 from [1].

(Schmidt, 1967) There exist constants \(C_1, C_2 > 0\) depending only on \(n,m,K\) such that \[\begin{equation} C_1 T^{n}\le \#\{S \in \mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Gr}}}(m,K^{n}) \ | \ H(S) \le T \} \le C_2 T^{n} \end{equation}\]
