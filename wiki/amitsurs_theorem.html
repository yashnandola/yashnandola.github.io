    Consider the following notation.

    • \(m,r \in \mathbb{N}\) are two coprime integers.
    • \(n = \mathop{\mathrm{ord}}_{m} r\) is the multiplicative order or \(r\) modulo \(m\), that is the smallest postive integer \(k\) such that \(m \mid r^{k}-1\).
    • \(s = \gcd(r-1,m)\).
    • \(t = m/s\).

    When \(r=1\), we will assume \(n=s=1\). In these definitions, we will think of \(m\) and \(r\) as two parameters and \(n,s,t\) will automatically be set as defined above.

    There is an auxilary object \(\mathfrak{U}_{m,r}\) that we are interested in.

    (Amitsur, 1955) Consider the following conditions on the numbers \(m,r,n,s,t\) defined above. Then \(\mathfrak{U}_{m,r}\) is a division algebra if and only if both and given below hold.

    1. One of the following two conditions hold.
      1. \(\gcd(n,t) = 1\). This implies that \(\gcd(s,t) = 1\).
      2. \(n=2n', m=2^{\alpha} m',s=2s'\), for some \(\alpha \ge 2\) and \(m',s',n'\) are odd numbers, such that \(\gcd(n,t)=\gcd(s,t)=2\) and \(2^{\alpha} \mid (r + 1)\).
    2. One of the following two conditions hold.
      1. \(n=s=2\) and \(m \mid (r +1)\).
      2. For every prime \(q \mid n\) there exists a prime \(p \mid m\) such that if \(m = p^{\alpha} m'\) with \(p \nmid m'\), we get \(q \nmid \mathop{\mathrm{ord}}_{m'} r\). In addition, at least one of the following must hold regarding each such \(p,q\).
        • \(p \neq 2\) and \(\gcd(q,\frac{ p^{ \delta} - 1}{s} ) = 1\), where \(\delta = \mathop{\mathrm{ord}}_{m'} p\).
        • \(p=q=2\) and \(m/4 \equiv \delta \equiv 1 \pmod{2}\), where \(\delta\) is as above.

    This can be used to then setup the following

    (Amitsur, 1955) The following is an exhaustive list of finite groups \(G_{0}\) that can be embedded in some \(\mathbb{Q}\)-division algebra \(D\).

    Group structure Conditions on the parameters Size of the group Dimension of the smallest division algebra containing the group
    \(G_{0} \subseteq D^{*}\) \(\#G_{0}\) \(\dim_{\mathbb{Q}} \mathbb{Q}\langle G_{0}\rangle\)
    \(\mathfrak{D}^{*}\) \(48\) \(16\)
    \(\mathfrak{I}^{*}\) \(120\) \(20\)
    \(G_{m,r}\) \(r \le m\) are coprime and the theorem above is satisfied by \(m\mathop{\mathrm{ord}}_{m} r\) \(\varphi(m) \mathop{\mathrm{ord}}_{m} r\)
    \((m,r,n,s,t) = (m,r,\mathop{\mathrm{ord}}_{m} r, \gcd(r-1,m),m/s)\)
    if \(r\neq 1\), otherwise it is \((m,1,1,1,m)\)
    \(\mathfrak{T}^{*} \times G_{m,r}\) \(m,r\) satisfy the same as above, \(m\) is odd and \(24 m \mathop{\mathrm{ord}}_{m} r\) \(4 \varphi(m)\mathop{\mathrm{ord}}_{m} r\)
    \(\mathop{\mathrm{ord}}_{m} 2\) is odd

    Here \(\mathfrak{T}^{*}, \mathfrak{D}^{*}, \mathfrak{I}^{*}\) are the binary tetrahedral group, binary octahedral group and binary icosahedral group respectively. They are finite groups whose respective size is 24,48 and 120.
