A particular non-commutative differential equation (NCDE) is a topic of research of GHED and HNM. Also see basic triangle theorem and iterated-integrals.

Let \(({\mathcal A},d)\) be a differential algebra (i.e.Â the ring of constants \(\ker(d)\) is a field) \(M_1,M_2\in {\mathcal A}_+\langle \!\langle X\rangle \!\rangle\) and consider the NCDE \[\begin{equation}\label{ncde1} S'=\mathbf{d}(S)=M_1S+SM_2 \end{equation}\] Then

  1. If \(d\) admits a section, there exists a solutions that satisfies \(\langle S\mid 1_{X^*} \rangle=1_{{\mathcal A}}\).
  2. For \(S\), solution of the above.
    1. The constant term \(\langle S\mid 1_{X^*} \rangle\) is a constant.
    2. If there exists a character \(\chi\in \Xi({\mathcal A})\) such that \(\underline{\chi}(S)=0\), then \(S=0\) . In particular if \(S_i,\ i=1,2\) are solutions such that \(\underline{\chi}(S_1)=\underline{\chi}(S_2)\), then they are equal
  3. The set of solutions is a \({\mathbb{C}}\)-vector space, is \[\begin{equation}\label{sol_ncde1} S_lC\langle \!\langle X\rangle \!\rangle S_r, \end{equation}\] where \(S_l\) and \(S_r\) satisfy \(S'=M_1S\) and \(S'=SM_2\) respectively.

Series of monomials

Suppose \(({\mathcal A},d)\) be a commutative differential algebra over a field of characteristic \(0\). Let \(X= \{ x_1,x_2,\cdots\}\) be a possibly infinite set of letters. Consider elements \(f_1,f_2,\cdots\in {\mathcal A}\) that are in bijection with \(X\).

Let \(\mathcal{M}\) be the free commutative monoid generated by \(X\) and suppose for \(x_i \in X\) and \(w \in \mathcal{M}\) we define \(\nu_{x_i}(w)\) to be the number of occurences of \(x_i\) in \(w\). Define a series \(S\in {\mathcal A}^\mathcal{M}\) given by \[\begin{align} \langle S\mid w \rangle = \prod_{i}\frac{ f_{i}^{\nu_{x_i}(w)}}{ \nu_{x_i}(w)!}. \end{align}\]

Then we can check that \(S\) satisfies \[\begin{align} \mathbf{d}(S) = M S. \end{align}\] where \(M = \sum_{i} d(f_i) x_i.\)
