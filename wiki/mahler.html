Mahler’s measure

Consider a polynomial \(p(z) \in \mathbb{C}[z]\) so that

\[\begin{align} p(z) = a(z-\alpha_1)(z-\alpha_2)\ldots (z-\alpha_n) . \end{align}\]

Then, the Mahler measure of this polynomial is defined as

\[\begin{align} M(p) = \exp\left( \int_{0}^{1} \left| p\left( e^{2 \pi i \theta} \right) \right| d\theta \right) = |a| \prod_{| \alpha_i| \ge 1} | \alpha_i| . \end{align}\]

For an algebraic integer \(\beta\), \(M(\beta)\) is the Mahler measure of the minimal polynomial of \(\beta\).

Lehmer’s conjecture

There exists constant \(\mu > 1\) such that either \(M(p)=1\) or \(M(p)\ge \mu\) for any irreducible \(p(z) \in \mathbb{C}[z]\) with integer coefficients.

Known results

(Dobrowolski, 1979, [1])

Let \(p(z) \in \mathbb{C}[z]\) and \(d = \deg p\). Then for large enough \(d\), \[\begin{align} \log M(p) \ge C \left( \frac{\log \log d}{\log d} \right)^{3} \end{align}\] for \(C > 1 - \varepsilon\) for any \(\varepsilon > 0\).

Improvements have been made on the constant.

Cyclotomic fields

For cyclotomic fields, the Lehmer’s conjecture has been solved. Theorem 5.39 in [2] states that

Suppose \(\beta\) is a an integer lying in \(\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{n})\) for some \(n\). Then, except when \(\beta = 1,2 \cos(2\pi/5) ,2\cos(2\pi/7)\) and \(2 \cos(2\pi/60)\) or their conjugates, we have that \[\begin{equation} \frac{\log M(\beta)}{\deg \beta} > \log 1.311703\dots = 0.27132\dots \end{equation}\]
