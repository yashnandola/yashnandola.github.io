Method of Lagrange Multipliers

Let \(f,g:\mathbb{R}^{d} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) be smooth functions. We want to find a point \(x^{*} \in \mathbb{R}^{d}\) such that \(g(x^{*})=0\) but \(f(x^{*})\) is the minimum possible with the constraint on \(g\).

Then the solution \(x^{*}\) will satisfy \[\begin{align} Df(x^{*}) = \lambda Dg(x^{*}). \end{align}\] for some \(\lambda \in \mathbb{R}\).
