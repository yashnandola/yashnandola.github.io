Topological Quantum Field Theory

A topological quantum field theory is basically a monoidal functor among the following monoidal categories.

\[\begin{align} F:\mathbf{Cob}_{d} \rightarrow \mathbf{Vec}_k \end{align}\]

Here \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Cob}}}_{d}\) is the category of closed compact manifolds of \(d-1\) dimensions upto diffeomorphisms where the monoidal product is direct sum and morphisms are cobordisms, that is \(d\)-dimensional manifold with boundary (whenever they exist) such that the boundary is the disjoint union of the endpoints. We add the empty manifold in \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Cob}}}_{d}\) that is the unit of the category.

\(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Vec}}}_{k}\) is just the monoidal category of \(k\)-vector spaces.

\(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Cob}}}_{2}\) is a category whose objects are just disjoint union of circles. The morphisms then look like pipes, pants, etc. For a 2-dimensional TQFT, having this functor is equivalent to having a Frobenious algebra, which is the image of a circle under the functor. What is a Frobeious algebra? This could be the definition!

Since each \(d\)-manifold is a cobordism between two empty sets and since empty set is mapped to the unit in \(\mathop{\mathrm{\mathbf{Vec}}}_{k}\) which is \(k\), a \(d\)-dimensional TQFT maps a \(d\)-dimensional manifold to a scalar multiplication map in \(k\). Hence, each TQFT gives a topological invariant of \(d\)-dimensional manifolds taking values in \(k\). Using this, one can recover the Jones polynomial as a 3-dimensional TQFT.

3-dimensional TQFT are related to Hopf-algebras, but not in a functorial manner. The one that recovers Jones polynomial is the universal algebra of \(\mathfrak{sl}_{2}(\mathbb{R})\).
